## Persona Definition: The Creative Problem Solver

They love the aha! moment — the satisfaction of making something work through clever arrangement, timing, and physics. They enjoy experimenting, tinkering, and discovering unexpected solutions that feel uniquely theirs.

“I’m the kind of person who can make anything work if you give me a few objects and some space to play.”

They want to feel:

- Smart
- Creative
- Resourceful
- Playful

Not stressed, not rushed. They want a sandbox that rewards curiosity.

## Demographic (general)

- **Age:** 18–40  
- **Gender:** Any  
- **Location:** Global, but especially strong in US, Europe, and Latin America  
- **Gaming Experience:** Casual to mid‑core  
- **Platform:** Mobile first, but also PC puzzle fans  


## What they enjoy

- Games that let them *experiment*  
- Light humor and charm  
- Physics toys, Rube Goldberg machines, chain reactions  
- Feeling clever without being punished for mistakes  
- Short sessions that still feel meaningful  

## What frustrates them

- Overly strict “one correct solution” puzzles  
- Timers or pressure  
- Sloppy physics that feel unfair  
- Visual clutter that hides important information  
- Tutorials that over-explain  

## What keeps them playing

- Discovering new objects and interactions  
- Levels that feel like little toys  
- Seeing their contraptions “come alive”  
- Sharing funny or unexpected solutions  
- A sense of progression without grind  


## Behavior in Roll’n’Snap

### How they approach puzzles

- They try the obvious solution first  
- Then they start experimenting  
- They enjoy breaking the intended path  
- They love when the game reacts in surprising ways  

### How they interact with objects

- They rotate everything “just to see what happens”  
- They test physics repeatedly  
- They enjoy stacking, balancing, and chaining effects  
- They treat objects like toys, not tools  

### How they play sessions

- 5–15 minutes at a time  
- Often while commuting, waiting, or relaxing  
- They replay levels to try new ideas  


## Art & Tone Fit

Hand‑drawn, silly, “Dumb Ways to Die” energy. Matches because 
persona does not need realism. They want *delight*.

- Charming, expressive objects  
- Little personality touches (googly eyes, tiny animations)  
- Humor that doesn’t interrupt gameplay  
- A world that feels like a playful desk or messy room  


## Adjacent Personas


## 1. The Tinkerer Kid-at-Heart

Loves toys, chain reactions, and silly physics. Plays for fun, not challenge.

## 2. The Completionist Engineer

Wants to optimize, perfect, and master every level. Loves optional challenges.

## 3. The Casual Relaxer

Plays to unwind. Prefers low pressure, cozy vibes, and simple solutions.

## 4. The Creator/Sharer

Wants to show off clever or funny solutions. Loves replayability and social features.


# Newzoo Persona Analysis 

## Best Fit: The Time Filler

### Why this is the strongest fit

Roll’n’Snap player:

- Plays in short bursts (5–15 minutes)
- Enjoys light, playful, low-pressure experiences
- Likes simple controls and quick iteration
- Wants to feel clever, not stressed
- Plays while commuting, waiting, relaxing

Aligns with The Time Filler, who:

- Plays to pass time
- Prefers mobile-friendly, easy-to-pick-up games
- Likes humor, charm, and low friction
- Doesn’t want long sessions or deep commitment
- Enjoys “toy-like” interactions and simple fun

Roll’n’Snap’s tone, art style, and physics‑toy gameplay are exactly what Time Fillers gravitate toward.

## Other Personas 

### Secondary Fit: The Cloud Gamer

- Likes accessible, low-commitment games
- Enjoys experimentation
- Prefers games that “just work”
- Doesn’t want to invest heavily in learning curves

Your Creative Problem Solver shares these traits, especially the desire for experimentation without punishment.

### Tertiary Fit: The Popcorn Gamer

A lighter match, but still relevant:
- They enjoy watching funny or surprising game moments
-  They like sharing clips
- They enjoy humor and spectacle

Game’s silly physics and “Dumb Ways to Die” tone can appeal to this group, especially through social media clips.

### Personas That Do Not Fit Well

This is important for marketing clarity.

Not a fit:
- The Ultimate Gamer → too hardcore
- The All-Round Enthusiast → too invested in deeper systems
- The Community Gamer → too socially driven
- The Hardware Enthusiast → irrelevant
- The Story-Driven Gamer → narrative isn’t the focus

Roll’n’Snap is not about mastery, competition, or narrative depth — it’s about playful creativity.
