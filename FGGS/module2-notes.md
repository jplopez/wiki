# Finishing Great Games That Sell: Module Summary

> **Topic:** Building a Sustainable and Successful Indie Game Business

## Overview

This module shifts your perspective on game development from a passion project to a viable business. It emphasizes financial planning, iterative development, and strategic validation of ideas.

- **Financial Sustainability:**  Critical for indie game developers, focusing on generating revenue to cover costs and potentially earn a living.
- **Realistic Targets:** Setting both a bare minimum income target and a desired goal to guide your planning and development.
- **Iterative Development:**  Embracing a process of building a game, testing it, and refining it based on feedback, rather than striving for a perfect initial product.

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| “Bare Minimum Wage” | The lowest income level needed to sustain oneself, considering personal circumstances. |
| “Monetization Strategies” | Encompass various methods for generating revenue from a game, such as unit sales, in-app purchases, and advertising. |
| “Platform Cut” | The percentage taken by digital distribution platforms (like Steam or consoles) from game sales revenue. |
| “Unfinished Awesome Games” | The dream game with IGF awards potential. |

## Recommendations & Advice

- Establish both a bare minimum income target and a realistic desired income goal. *(This ensures you have a clear financial roadmap and understand the scale of your ambition.)*
- Perform a simple math exercise to determine the number of in-app purchases or ads needed to achieve your financial goals. *(This provides a tangible understanding of the revenue required for success.)*
- Focus on the process of shipping games and building a fan base. *(Prioritizing delivery and community engagement is key to long-term success.)*
- Don’t get fixated on creating a single, perfect game; embrace iterative development. *(This reduces the risk of over-investment and allows for greater responsiveness to player feedback.)*
- Build a sustainable business model to increase your odds of success. *(A sustainable model ensures long-term viability beyond the initial game release.)*
- Pick one hypothesis to validate at a time. *(This allows for focused testing and efficient resource allocation.)*
- Focus on speed and being straight to the point. *(Prioritizing rapid iteration and testing minimizes wasted effort.)*
- Don’t try to do everything at once. *(Avoid overwhelming yourself with ambitious goals and maintain a manageable scope.)*
- Leverage existing audiences and events (Game Gems, Casual Games, Fan Games). *(Utilizing established communities reduces marketing costs and increases visibility.)*
- Build up on initial ideas. *(Expanding upon promising concepts maximizes the potential for success.)*
- Test mechanics to ensure they work. *(Validating core mechanics early prevents costly rework later.)*
- Take a break from the project – engage in a hobby, work on a different project, or play games in the genre to regain inspiration. *(Stepping away can provide fresh perspective and renewed motivation.)*
- If anxiety prevents you from working on the game, work on a smaller project to manage anxiety and maintain a sense of progress. *(Addressing mental health is crucial for sustained productivity.)*
- Playing games in the genre you're developing can help you reconnect with the initial motivation and generate new ideas. *(Immersion in the genre reinforces your vision and sparks creativity.)*
- Don’t be afraid to temporarily “pause” or “suspend” a project if it’s overwhelming; it’s not a failure. *(Recognizing limitations and adjusting your approach is a sign of smart development.)*
- Use hiatus periods to gain experience, earn money, or explore different genres, which can inform your return to the original project. *(Taking advantage of downtime expands your skillset and broadens your creative horizons.)*

## Examples & Case Studies

- **Reveal the Deep:** A two-hour pixel art game sold for $1-$2 on Steam, with over 100,000 copies sold, demonstrating the potential for monetization even with a simple game. *(This illustrates the viability of low-cost, focused games.)*
- The vast majority of mobile games fail to generate substantial revenue. *(Highlighting the competitive landscape and the need for strategic planning.)*
- 500 new games released *every day* on the App Store (2015 data). *(Demonstrates the high volume of competition in the mobile market.)*
- Multimedia Fusion vs. Unity – The speaker’s experience developing games on Multimedia Fusion and later transitioning to Unity. *(Illustrates the importance of engine selection and the potential for skill development.)*
- The difficulty of building 3D games in Multimedia Fusion compared to Unity. *(Reinforces the need to choose an engine suited to the project’s scope and complexity.)*

## Summary

This module focuses on transforming indie game development from a passion-driven dream into a sustainable business. It emphasizes setting realistic financial targets, utilizing iterative development techniques like “Tactical Small Games” to validate ideas, and prioritizing shipping games to build a fan base.  Key strategies include understanding platform cuts, focusing on genre mastery, and embracing a pragmatic approach to game development – “Engineering the outcome.”  Ultimately, success hinges on a disciplined approach to financial planning, combined with a commitment to continuous improvement and a deep understanding of player expectations.
