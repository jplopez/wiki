# Foundations of Finishing Great Games that Sell (FGGS)

> **Source videos:** `Module 1` (10 video(s))  
> **Topic:** Laying the groundwork for successful indie game development and marketing by avoiding common pitfalls, leveraging structured methodologies, and strategically planning for long-term success.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Hope Marketing:** Relying on hope rather than deliberate planning to achieve marketing success.  
- **Hope Game Dev:** Developing a prototype or mechanic with the hope it can later be expanded into a full game, often leading to unsustainable costs.  
- **Engineering Success:** A deliberate, optimized approach to game development and marketing to increase the probability of success.  
- **FDGS Framework:** A structured methodology for finishing high-quality games that sell, with tools and checklists for each stage of development.  
- **Survivorship Bias:** A cognitive bias where only successful examples are considered, ignoring the broader context of failures.  
- **Filters:** Mechanisms like media outlets and social media algorithms that determine which games gain visibility.  
- **Reverse Engineering Game Success:** Analyzing successful games to identify the principles and strategies behind their success.  
- **Hit Ratio:** The proportion of games in a specific style that achieve success compared to the total produced.  
- **Promotion:** The act of communicating or getting the word out about your game.  
- **Marketing Material:** The embodiment of the message you are trying to promote, such as trailers, press kits, and landing pages.  
- **530 Close Rule:** A method to structure communication effectively to maintain attention and explain a game's value.  
- **Game Surface:** The outward-facing aspects of a game that make it appealing and marketable.  
- **Core of the Game:** The deeper elements of a game, such as story and progression, appreciated after player commitment.  
- **Attention Magnets:** Features or elements of a game designed to attract and retain player interest.  
- **Production Hacks:** Efficient methods to maximize results with minimal effort during development.  
- **ProDesign:** A process combining production and design to balance cost and impact.  
- **Development Timeline Algorithm:** A systematic approach to validate and optimize a game at each stage of development.  
- **Baseline Marketing:** A fundamental promotional strategy to ensure a game reaches its audience effectively.  
- **Career Algorithm:** A strategy for creating a sequence of games aligned with long-term career goals.  
- **Dream Game:** The ultimate game a developer aspires to create.  
- **Dream Career:** The ideal career path, including work environment, studio size, and types of games developed.  
- **Career Optimization:** Strategically planning steps to achieve one's dream game and career.  
- **Monetizing the Process:** Generating income while developing skills and technology through smaller, strategic projects.

## Recommendations & Advice

- Avoid hope marketing and hope game dev by planning deliberately and validating ideas early. *(context: to prevent unsustainable costs and failure)*  
- Follow the FDGS framework's structured steps and checklists to optimize development and marketing. *(context: ensures a systematic approach to success)*  
- Be aware of survivorship bias and analyze both successful and failed games for a broader understanding. *(context: prevents misleading conclusions)*  
- Start marketing early in development to avoid last-minute struggles. *(context: ensures better preparation and results)*  
- Use the 530 close rule to create compelling marketing materials and pitches. *(context: helps maintain attention and communicate value)*  
- Design the game's surface deliberately to maximize its appeal and marketability. *(context: first impressions are crucial for attracting players)*  
- Use attention magnets and production hacks to create an efficient and marketable game. *(context: increases appeal while minimizing effort)*  
- Validate game ideas, attention magnets, and promotional strategies early in development. *(context: avoids costly mistakes later)*  
- Plan game development as part of a long-term career strategy. *(context: aligns projects with career goals)*  
- Monetize skill and technology development through smaller, sellable projects. *(context: generates income while building toward the dream game)*

## Examples & Case Studies

- **Edmund McMillian:** Struggled with marketing early in his career before achieving success with *Super Meat Boy* and *The Binding of Isaac*. *(illustrates the importance of learning marketing)*  
- **Ryan Clark:** Estimated that 95% of indie games are not profitable. *(highlights the challenges of indie game success)*  
- **Jake Burkett:** Noted the high failure rate of games on Steam, with many projects never being finished. *(emphasizes the importance of completing games)*  
- **Crypt of the Necrodancer:** Used a pun name and targeted overlapping audiences of rhythm and roguelike games. *(example of using attention magnets effectively)*  
- **Nintendo's Development Process:** Demonstrates ProDesign by optimizing resources for maximum impact. *(example of balancing cost and quality)*  
- **Steampie Sales Data:** Used to evaluate potential audiences and extract actionable insights. *(example of leveraging data for development and marketing)*

## Summary

This module introduces the foundational principles of the FGGS framework, emphasizing the importance of deliberate planning, structured methodologies, and strategic marketing in indie game development. It highlights common pitfalls like hope marketing and survivorship bias, while offering actionable advice on creating marketable games and aligning projects with long-term career goals. By focusing on attention magnets, production hacks, and early validation, developers can optimize their chances of success and build sustainable careers.
